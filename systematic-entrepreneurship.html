Supporting the next generation of African entrepreneurs

In this project, we created a platform that provided entrepreneurs with a step by step process for developing their idea, along with community, mentors and content to help them stay on track

The challenge before us

According to statista.com, as of 2020, the average startup failure rate in Africa stood at 54 percent. This failure rate went higher during the covid pandemic.

This was a very devastating fact considering that most graduates in Kenya and Africa as a whole defaulted to entrepreneurship due to the high unemployment rate(around around 13 percent according to TradingEconomics.com).

We sort out to understand why startups failed at this high rate, and what could be possibly done about it

The Process

Market Research

In this stage, the whole team(marketers, developers and the product manager) set out to understand the main challenges entrepreneurs were facing, collecting the different needs they had.

Curating Interview and Survey Questions

We started the research process by identifying the main insights and assumptions we wanted to validate from the market, which included the following:

The main challenges they experienced

The entrepreneurship process they followed

Where they got their entrepreneurship content from

We then curated questions we would ask in the field and in the surveys

Participants

After curating the questions, we then identified the people we would be contacting. These would mainly consist of founders of small businesses in different stages of a business.

Having founders at different stages would allow us to get unbiased feedback. We built a list of 20 founders in the Kenyan ecosystem.

Interviews

We entered the next important phase; user interviews. We divided our team of five to do the interviews of different people in order to cover more ground.

The interviews were mainly primarily conduced virtually since they were done during the pandemic.

These were some of the statistics from the interviews:

What were the main challenges faced by entrepreneurs?

The main challenges they experienced

Lack of mentorship from seasoned entrepreneurs

Where they got their entrepreneurship content from

Lack of accountability and keeping track of tasks

Ideas, ideas, ideas

After discovering the main needs the market had, we dived into the next phase, which was ideation. We brainstormed ideas for potential solutions. The following questions guided our process:

  • How might we provide them with the entrepreneurship knowledge they need at each stage?
  • How might we link seasoned entrepreneurs to the novice ones for mentorship and guidance?
  • How might we help them be more accountable in carrying out their tasks?

Outlook of proposed solution

After going through our different ideas, we voted on the top priorities which we saw should exist in the solution we builded. These were the pillars we decided to settle on:

Provide knowledge and resources

Provide a framework for entrepreneurs to follow

Provide a community of both mentors and other entreprenuers to provide support

After identifiying what areas we wanted to focus on, we then set out to create the solution for entrepreneurs. This was our solution statement:

A platform that provides entrepreneurs with a step by step process for developing your idea, along with community, mentors and content to help you stay on track

Creating a platform to support entrepreneurs

1. Who are we building for?

It was vital for us to define who we were building for since during market research we identified different market segments who had different requirements in mind.

From the data we collected, we concluded that recent graduates in the ideation phase were the largest market base. They were also more capable of paying for the product given most of them were employed and had sources of income

2. Whats the user’s journey

Having identified our main user, we started sketching out the different screens our user would interact with.

We started with paper sketches, which we later translated into user journeys on both milanote and figjam.

We used Milanote to document the user flows of users; both new and returning users. We did this in several iterations, building on all ideas each member had to bring.

3. Into the wireframes...

Having an idea of what the user journey would look like, we set out to design the wireframes that guide the look of the platform. We used figma, and i took leadership of this process. I planned the wireframing sprints and led the sessions where the whole team was involved.

4. High level designs

In this phase, the team handed over the wireframes to the visual designer who converted them into high level designs. This process was very iterative and it involved a lot of collaboration.

5. Testing

We then moved to the next phase of the process, which invloved getting feedback on the visual designs before moving to the development stage.

We targeted to get feedback from our main stakeholders which included:

  • Team Management
  • Pool of potenital users within our user persona
  • Mentors we had contacted, who were seasoned entrepreneurs

We shared the designs with them and conducted virtual calls and then observed them as they used the platform.

In these tests, we sought to determine the designs performance across the following metrics:

  • User interface
  • Ease of navigation
  • Relevance of content provided
  • Solving the challenge

6. Test insights and iterations

From the tests, it was clear that the usablity and user interface was performing well, but the content was not up to par yet. Most users complained that the educational content(which was mainly in video form) was not localised to the Kenyan and African audience. Examples included of foreign startups which did not have the same economic environment as Kenyan startups did.

Using this feedback, we came up with a couple of iterations to be done both at the time, and others in the next versions of the platform:

  • To change the examples to kenyan ones
  • To develop our own video content instead of third party content.

7. Development handover

After the design iterations were made, the designs were then handed over the development team, consisting of one backend developer and myself.

The designer shared the figma prototype and the resources in the designs(colors, fonts, images etc.)

I oversee the development process by doing the following tasks:

  1. Creating the system design and system architecture
  2. Deciding the tech stack to be used
  3. Creating roadmaps for the team
  4. Planning for and overseeing weekly sprints
  5. Front-end development using Reactjs
  6. Back-end development using Django

The designers and developers had weekly meetings to oversee any problems with translation into code and for QA.

Launch, and initial feedback

With a live platform, we proceeded to do a grand launch to our market. The initial feedback was good, having over 200 signups in just 3 days.

However, as more users got to use our product, we realized that there were some issues with our approach to the problem. The challenges included the following:

Lack of curated content:

The market was telling us that the content was not curated enough for their exact challenges, and it was a bit too theoretical.

We needed to come up with more practical ways to instill the knowledge

Cost:

Through user feedback, we realized that most entrepreneurs struggled to pay for the platform using the chosen model. This was obviously a challenge we needed to address because we needed to meet organizational goals.

We needed to figure out a model that gave us recurring income, which also involved rethinking our target audience.

Low Engagement:

Despite breaking down the content into small chunks they could absorb, entrepreneurs were struggling to assign timelines to their tasks, sometimes completely forgetting to do the task.

We needed a way to improve how the entrepreneurs used our platform

The Pivot

Having observed the recurring challenges from the MVP, as well as from feedback from management, we decided to switch up our offering from a self paced platform, to an acceletator that would run for 12 weeks.

This is how the accelerator would address the discovered challenges:

Practical Content:
We would script, record and create content that would provide practical tools to allow entrepreneurs to learn execution skills.

Niche Market:
We decided to target a more niche market; entreprenuers who were working on their ventures full time. This would allow us to work with entrepreneurs who were more committed and more likely to pay

Clear Timelines:
We broke down the content into 12 weeks, meaning the entrepresnuers knew what to work on each week, and had clear deadlines that allow them to fast track their journey.

The impact

Through our platform, over 10 entreprenurs were able to launch their startups and get to traction.

The product went through one more pivot, focusing on selling to corporates and schools, and it eventually became its own company, now known as Kinetic

Major lessons learned

  1. Importance of documentation:
    During the process of research and coming up with the platform, we did not the best documentation of insights, processes, roadmaps etc. This meant that we could not make the data led decisions because there was no data. Bringing on additional members to the team was also difficult since there was nothing for them to go through. I learnt to consider documentation from the word go.
  2. Incorporating business goals:
    We unfortunately considered the business goals such as revenue and culture too late into the process, and therefore those goals fought quite a bit with the user goals in the long run. Considering the organizational goals is also very important at the start.
  3. Spending more time in design:
    I believe we rushed through the design phase and it later affected us in the development process; many changes and updates were being done during development which indicated that we spent little time in testing at the design phase. Having enough feedback and passing criterias that would indicate it was time to handover to deveopers is very important.
